Symphony Strips

A start up from SHAD Carleton 2022

Symphony Strips

Project Description

During the summer of 2022, I was fortunate enough to attend Shad Carleton where a group of fellow students and I (Design Group 2) were given an entrepreneurship challenge with the theme of "wellness". Design Group 2 and I engineered the "Symphony Strip" prototype, which is a strip of ultrasonic sensors wired to an Arduino microcontroller with piezo speakers to play musical notes. The ingenuity behind Symphony Strips is the strip of sensors can be placed along stairwells or hallways, and as someone walks by the row of sensors, series of musical notes will play!

Hottie Cooler

Project Description

"Are You a Hotty Then Cool Down Your Body" is a funny smart fan project made for the JAM Hacks 2023, hackathon! It uses a temperature sensor to control the speed of the fan, which gets TAPED onto your laptop... very subtly. The smart fan can also be controlled either through a Node.js website or an IR remote controller connected to an Arduino. We also had an LCD display to show the temperature and humidity, as well as LEDs to represent the different fan speeds.

2nd place for KuriusHacks hackathon.

Pygame Food Game

Project Description

The Pygame Food Game is meant to be an interactive teaching tool targeted at younger audiences to help educate the youth on the issues surrounding food insecurity. The game itself is a mix between "Cookie Clicker" and "Plague Inc.", where the user is challenged with feeding the starving world. There are 4 different power-ups with info cards to further explain how each power-up can be applied in the real world. There are also 4 different disasters that make the game more challenging and realistic.
